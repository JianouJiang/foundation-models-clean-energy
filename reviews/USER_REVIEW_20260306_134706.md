## TWO CRITICAL LAYOUT PROBLEMS

### Problem 1: Line Numbers Must Be Removed
The manuscript currently shows line numbers (every 5th line) in the margins. This is caused by:
- `\usepackage{lineno}`
- `\modulolinenumbers[5]`
- `\linenumbers`

**These must be removed.** No published journal paper has line numbers. They are only for review submissions. This paper is for AIDER journal — we want it to look like a finished, professional publication.

**Fix:** Remove or comment out `\usepackage{lineno}`, `\modulolinenumbers[5]`, and `\linenumbers`.

### Problem 2: Figures Are All Pushed to the End of the Paper
All figures appear at the very end of the manuscript instead of inline with the text. This is caused by the `review` option in `\documentclass[preprint,12pt,review]{elsarticle}`. The `review` option activates the `endfloat` behavior.

**Fix:** Change the document class to:
```latex
\documentclass[preprint,12pt]{elsarticle}
```
Remove the `review` option. This will allow figures to appear where they are referenced in the text using `[t]`, `[h]`, `[htbp]` etc.

Additionally, check all `\begin{figure}` environments and ensure they use `[htbp]` placement instead of just `[t]`, to give LaTeX more flexibility in placing them near the referencing text.

### Journal Target
The `\journal{Renewable and Sustainable Energy Reviews}` should be changed to `\journal{AI-Driven Energy Research}` — this paper is for AIDER, not RSER.
